Introduction

There is imperfection in an animals ability to perceive physical reality and their cognitive processes and this inconsistency can lead to incorrect assessments in prey choice. Specifically, proportional processing, the ability to assess differences between quantities, can be constrained. This was tested in Trachops cirrhosus, given various quantities of frog choruses. Bats genreally perferred larger chorus sizes, but failed to discern the differences in frog chorus sizes in trails between larger choruses (i.e.choruses of 5 and 6 frogs) vs.smaller ones (i.e.1 and 2 frogs). Thus, this experiments determined that T. cirrhosus relay on the relative differences and not the absolute differences in choruses and may be less persnickety when foraging in larger chorusing groups of frogs.

Visualization of Data

Calculated absolute & relative difference in call number (absolute diff/mean(number of calls of the twos stimuli))

Fitted two generalized linear mixed models (binomial) with absolute diff & relative diff as predictor variables (packages used: {lme4}) & individual bats were included as a random factor

The two models above (as well as a null model with random variable bat) were compared based on AIC values

Compared linear model to a quadratic polynomial model using F test
